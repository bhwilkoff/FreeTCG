## The 30-Virtue Canon ("The Detoxing Man Is...")

Each virtue is a single adjective with a parenthetical clarifier. The structure is non-negotiable to preserve — the parenthetical is where the design tension lives. Below: the canonical list with extracted lived meaning, identified tensions, and design hooks.

### 1. Kind (of heart and mind)
**Core meaning.** Kindness is *both* outward (heart — empathy, building others up) *and* inward (mind — refusing self-shame, treating yourself as your best self). It's not always achievable; the Detoxing Man strives, and looks for new ways.
**Tension.** External kindness without internal kindness collapses into performance.
**Card-design hook.** Reward both outward generosity and self-forgiveness; reduce penalties for trying and failing.

### 2. Attuned (to the needs of others and the needs of the moment)
**Core meaning.** Listen first for what others need without imposing your will, then listen FOR what's possible when you harmonize. Like tuning a guitar — find harmony, don't force conformity.
**Tension.** Listening first does NOT mean passivity — you still advocate for what's possible.
**Card-design hook.** Resonance: cards that scale based on which other card types are in play.

### 3. Sincere (in word and deed)
**Core meaning.** No gap between what you say and what you do. Sincerity is the absence of performance.
**Card-design hook.** Symmetric effects — cost and benefit aligned. Flavor text and mechanics that reinforce each other.

### 4. Present (in both actions and feelings)
**Core meaning.** Not just physically there. Present means processing the feelings actually happening, sitting with discomfort instead of numbing it. (AirPods Pro as the modern metaphor for opting out.)
**Tension.** Being present to feelings is uncomfortable — the opposite of the productive distraction modern life sells.
**Card-design hook.** Active decisions every turn; cards that reward attention over autopilot.

### 5. Connected (by shared experience, passion, and expression)
**Core meaning.** Connection is built — through shared work, shared joy, shared voice. It's not given.
**Card-design hook.** Synergy mechanics that require two players' cards to be in shared zones.

### 6. Trusted (to root out injustice wherever it is found)
**Core meaning.** Privilege is unearned; use it to expand the world for others. *"If I cannot extend the world into which I was born to include marginalized populations, I should not be given the power to make important decisions."*
**Card-design hook.** Asymmetric power: cards that grant advantage must also grant advantage to others. The cost of leadership is responsibility.

### 7. Active (in body and mind)
**Core meaning.** Not "fitness" — relational aliveness. Walking a kid to school is presence + conversation + thought. The space activity creates is for *possibility*, not productivity.
**Card-design hook.** Movement / position / progression mechanics; paired cards for shared activity.

### 8. Empathetic (beyond those who look and think like him)
**Core meaning.** Expand the circle. Default kindness to strangers. *"My circle of empathy is big enough for the people I will fight with because they are trying to oppress... when we are all in the circle, there is no us and them."*
**Card-design hook.** Cross-faction synergies. Cards that reward including the unlike.

### 9. Desirous (with authenticity and integrity)
**Core meaning.** Wanting is OK — denial is the toxic response. Desire pursued with honesty and accountability is a virtue, not a vice.
**Card-design hook.** Reward declared intentions; "play into your stated direction" effects.

### 10. Willing (to wonder, to change, and to be wrong)
**Core meaning.** Willingness is both stance (open) and action (changing). Being wrong publicly is a feature.
**Card-design hook.** Effects that change a previous decision, retroactively or forward.

### 11. Open (to difference)
**Core meaning.** Difference is welcome, not tolerated. The default is curiosity.
**Card-design hook.** Cards that gain power when paired with cards from a *different* faction.

### 12. Capable (of love, of complexity, of listening)
**Core meaning.** Capability is multiplicity. Holding more than one feeling, more than one truth, more than one voice.
**Card-design hook.** Multi-tag cards; cards that work in multiple roles.

### 13. Unfinished (with himself and with his relationships)
**Core meaning.** Being finished = stagnation. Unfinished is the joy of play, not the tyranny of winning. *"Being unfinished is way more interesting than being done."*
**Tension.** Many men are taught to seek "arrived" states — settled job, finished house, earned respect. The framework rejects "arrived."
**Card-design hook.** Cards never reach a final form. Power scales open-endedly. No "end state."

### 14. Patient (with time and attention)
**Core meaning.** Patience is *attention management*, not time management. You can't always control time, but you can choose where your focus goes. *"An infinite line is hard to wait in. It is hard to have patience for infinity when time is so frustratingly finite."*
**Tension.** Patience with attention is a daily practice; patience with time is a fantasy.
**Card-design hook.** Cards that reward deliberate, incremental play; "Attention" as the resource (not Time).

### 15. Introspective (often, and without provocation)
**Core meaning.** Self-examination doesn't wait for crisis. It is regular practice.
**Card-design hook.** Effects that read your own zone or hand and grant rewards for self-knowledge.

### 16. Loving (in the open and in private)
**Core meaning.** Love performed publicly AND love practiced privately. Both, not either.
**Card-design hook.** Cards with a public effect and a private effect — both fire.

### 17. Conscious (of bias within himself)
**Core meaning.** Bias is internal first, external later. Recognizing it in yourself is upstream of fixing it anywhere else.
**Card-design hook.** Cards that ask you to declare an assumption before resolving.

### 18. Engaged (by courageous acts of self-discovery)
**Core meaning.** Self-discovery is the work; engagement is what makes it possible. Both noun and verb.
**Card-design hook.** Cards that activate when you take a risk — venture beyond your zone, play into uncertainty.

### 19. Responsible (for both intended and unintended consequences of his actions)
**Core meaning.** Accountability extends past intent. The harm you didn't mean is still harm.
**Card-design hook.** Cards that have a positive intended effect and a small negative side-effect; both must be accepted.

### 20. Collaborative (by default)
**Core meaning.** Default to working *with* rather than *over.* Solo work is a fallback, not the norm.
**Card-design hook.** Cards that bonus when played jointly with opponent's cards (shared work).

### 21. Resilient (in the face of failure and feeling)
**Core meaning.** Resilience is bouncing back from BOTH failure (external) and feeling (internal). Hard things and tender things equally.
**Card-design hook.** Recovery effects; second chances; cards that come back from discard.

### 22. Adaptable (of spirit and intention)
**Core meaning.** Adaptability is changing your spirit AND your intention — not just your behavior.
**Card-design hook.** Cards that transform between turns; cards whose effect shifts with the board state.

### 23. Supportive (of identity and growth)
**Core meaning.** Support what others are becoming, not just what they are now.
**Card-design hook.** Buff effects that reward partner cards' future plays, not current state.

### 24. Caring (for others and himself)
**Core meaning.** Self-care is foundational — you can't care for others without it. Steven Coriell's contribution: *"You cannot fully love others until you first love yourself. This is not a selfish statement."*
**Card-design hook.** Heal-self effects that also heal allies. Boundaries as care, not selfishness.

### 25. Communicative (of purpose and emotion)
**Core meaning.** Both — purpose (where you're going) and emotion (how you feel). Many men can do one and not the other.
**Card-design hook.** Cards that announce themselves before resolving; declare-then-do effects.

### 26. Grateful (of both effort and success)
**Core meaning.** Gratitude is for the trying, not just the achievement. Effort gets witnessed.
**Card-design hook.** Bonus rewards for *attempted* plays even if blocked.

### 27. Respectful (of expertise and of trauma)
**Core meaning.** Respect what others know AND what others have survived. Both are forms of authority.
**Card-design hook.** Cards that defer to other cards' parenthetical tags before resolving.

### 28. Curious (of ideas and people)
**Core meaning.** Curiosity outward AND inward — about ideas and the humans who hold them.
**Card-design hook.** Card-draw effects coupled with peek-at-opponent effects.

### 29. Purposeful (even if only in discovering his purpose)
**Core meaning.** Having a purpose is good; *seeking* one counts. The search is purposeful too.
**Card-design hook.** Cards that activate "in pursuit of" goals you haven't yet achieved.

### 30. Equitable (in opportunity and outcome)
**Core meaning.** Both. Opportunity *and* outcome — not just access, not just results. Hard to do both.
**Card-design hook.** Effects that level the playing field both at start and finish.

## Founding Philosophy (verbatim from `/who-is-the-detoxing-man/`)

> "We are In-Progress. We are Striving. We are Pursuing and Building and Questing toward authenticity and away from toxicity. These are the adjectives of those who choose to make progress, the men who are not satisfied with the masculinity they have been given. If Detoxing is a process, these are the men who have fully embraced it."

> Tagline: **"No One Way To Be a Man."**

> "Beyond simple definition, though, we recognize that we will never be fully 'Detoxed.' We are In-Progress."

**For Dad TCG:** the *In-Progress* framing is the most important takeaway. Cards should feel aspirational, not prescriptive. Mechanics should reward trying, not arrival. There is no "complete" deck.

## Voice Sources — Philosophy Posts

### "Soft Masculinity"
The unsubscribe metaphor: traditional masculine ideology = spam you didn't ask for. Soft masculinity = clicking unsubscribe, finding the tiny link in the fine print. *"Every time I hit unsubscribe from toxic behavior, my day gets that much better."*
**For Dad TCG voice:** small subversions. Cards that feel like opting out of an old script.

### "Loving Yourself Is Not a Selfish Act" (Steven Coriell Jr.)
A bisexual cop in the South deconstructing faith and self-acceptance to be a better parent. *"Love people more authentically... It doesn't come from a place of duty."*
**For Dad TCG voice:** self-care as foundation, not weakness. Boundaries as care.

### "For Anyone Who Is Sad"
Liminal, intimate. *"You can feel progress, movement, momentum. It is hard to generate, but it is not hard to feel."* The post sits in not-wrecked-not-thriving and validates the in-between.
**For Dad TCG voice:** card text can sit in the middle. "Stuck" can be a power, not a problem.

### "Precarious Masculinity: How Men Tell on Themselves"
Five-part analysis of fragile masculinity (Sexuality, Fidelity, Strength, Status, Safety). The fragile man "lashes out... to stop it from spreading, while somehow not understanding that with every comment he helps the video reach more people."
**For Dad TCG voice:** the contrast between secure love and precarious masculinity. Don't moralize against precariousness; show secure masculinity at work.

### "Feeling, Finding — A Core Practice"
Naming the practice of feeling-as-discovery. Most men were taught to suppress; the practice is to feel what's there and let it inform you.
**For Dad TCG voice:** cards that reward processing rather than skipping.

### "Subtle Shifts"
Change is rarely dramatic. The shifts that compound matter most.
**For Dad TCG voice:** small, repeated, additive effects. Nothing flashy.

## Cultural Criticism — Virtue Identification in Media

The "Detoxing the Movies," "Marvel Masculinity," and "Masculinity of Music" series each apply the 30-virtue framework to a specific work. Useful for Dad TCG because they show what *positive masculinity in narrative* looks like — characters / songs that DON'T preach but DO model.

Notable framings:
- **Steve Rogers (Endgame):** Trusted, Sincere, Capable, Resilient. The dance at the end is Loving (in the open and in private).
- **Thor & Hulk (Ragnarok):** Unfinished, Adaptable. The whole movie is a man rebuilding identity after loss.
- **Big Hero Six:** Caring, Supportive. Baymax as the externalized practice of care.
- **Mumford & Sons "Little Lion Man":** Responsible, Communicative (of emotion). The chorus is a confession.
- **Petey "Don't Tell the Boys":** Open, Conscious (of bias within himself). Acknowledging vulnerability against type.
- **Front Bottoms "Twin Size Mattress":** Connected, Resilient. Friendship as both fragile and survivable.
- **slimdan "Problem Solver":** Capable, Caring. The man who shows up for the breakdown.
- **Nikola Jokić:** Active, Patient, Collaborative. The basketball player whose game is modeled on shared joy.

**For Dad TCG:** card art prompts (later) and archetype reference inspirations live here. Each character cited is a potential archetype seed or a flavor-text reference. (Don't name them on cards — too licensing-fraught — but the *spirit* informs.)

## Recommended Faction Structure (drawn from extraction)

The 30 virtues are too many to be factions. Cluster them into 5-6 by relational similarity. Two viable cluster sets:

### Option A — Use 5 of the actual virtue adjectives as faction names
Pick 5 single-word adjectives that span the framework. Each becomes a faction; the remaining 25 virtues become *tags* on individual cards within each faction.

- **Present** (umbrella for Present, Sincere, Caring, Loving)
- **Connected** (umbrella for Connected, Attuned, Empathetic, Open, Collaborative, Communicative)
- **Unfinished** (umbrella for Unfinished, Willing, Engaged, Curious, Adaptable, Introspective)
- **Patient** (umbrella for Patient, Resilient, Grateful, Capable, Supportive)
- **Trusted** (umbrella for Trusted, Responsible, Equitable, Conscious, Respectful, Purposeful, Active, Desirous)

(Trusted is heavy — it's the "doing the work" cluster. Active and Desirous could move elsewhere.)

### Option B — Six clusters with general-noun names
- **Presence** (Present, Sincere, Kind, Loving)
- **Connection** (Connected, Attuned, Empathetic, Open, Collaborative)
- **Growth** (Unfinished, Willing, Engaged, Curious, Adaptable, Introspective)
- **Agency** (Active, Capable, Communicative, Desirous, Purposeful)
- **Integrity** (Trusted, Responsible, Conscious, Respectful, Equitable)
- **Resilience** (Resilient, Patient, Caring, Supportive, Grateful)

A is more on-brand (uses the framework's actual adjectives; preserves the parentheticals). B is cleaner mechanically.

## What NOT to lift

- **Anti-something framing.** "Detoxing" is a reaction-against. Dad TCG is FOR positive masculinity. The extraction keeps the substance and drops the "detox" label.
- **Community / WordPress meta.** Membership tiers, badges, forum mechanics — not relevant.
- **Academic deconstructive language.** The framework is conversational and lived; preserve that, not the more theoretical asides.
- **Explicit critique of named real people / movements.** The "Precarious Masculinity" essay calls out fragile-male behavior; for Dad TCG card content we model secure masculinity, we don't satirize the alternative.
- **Anything that reads as scolding the player.** The cards should *invite* the player into virtues, not lecture them about toxic patterns.
