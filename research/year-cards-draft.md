# Dad TCG — Year Cards Draft v0.1

*Date: 2026-05-08. Status: 28 sample Year cards drafted across all tonal modes. Full deck of 50 (per Decision 020) will be authored in M1; this draft establishes voice, structural templates, and mechanical scope.*

The Year deck is a **shared anthological deck** (D020). Both players share a single Year deck of ~50 cards. At start of each turn, the next Year card flips face-up and its modifier affects both players for that turn. Each match draws 18 Years from the larger pool — same structure, new story every time.

Year cards represent **what's happening this Year of dad-life** — could be a kid moment, could be the dad's own life, could be a community ritual, could be a season. They're anthological (Decision 020): not a chronological childhood arc; a year of a generation lived as a good father.

---

## Card structure

Each Year card has:

- **Name** — usually article + noun ("The Saturday Pancake," "Hot Lava Living Room")
- **Flavor** — one sentence in Rapid Reflection voice (the run-on naming a familiar moment, ending on a quiet beat)
- **Modifier** — small mechanical effect that fires this turn for both players
- **Optional Moment trigger** — a named Moment captured to the Highlight Reel if conditions match

Modifiers are deliberately *small* (single-stat shifts, +1/-1 cost adjustments, conditional draws, Moment captures). Year cards should color the turn, not dominate it.

## Joyful Years (8 cards — bright, comic, absurd-warm)

### 1. The Saturday Pancake
- **Flavor:** *"Burned the first one. Got the second one right. The kid ate three."*
- **Modifier:** First card you play in Your Family this turn captures a SATURDAY PANCAKE Moment.
- **Storytelling source:** Sunday-comics three-panel rhythm; the bumbler-with-accountability voice; mode #4 (the dad with the specific weird hobby).

### 2. Hot Lava Living Room
- **Flavor:** *"The couch is the only safe place. Has been since you were four."*
- **Modifier:** Play-tagged cards entering any zone this turn capture a HOT LAVA Moment. (One Moment per player max.)
- **Storytelling source:** Mode #1 (color commentary). Calvin & Hobbes adjacent.

### 3. The Surprise Trip
- **Flavor:** *"'Get in the car.' 'Where are we going?' 'You'll see.'"*
- **Modifier:** Once this turn, either player may move one of their cards from any zone to any other zone, free of cost. If both players move a card this turn, both capture a SURPRISE TRIP Moment.
- **Storytelling source:** The Adventurer archetype; mode #1 + #11 (the road-trip dad).

### 4. Sunday Comics
- **Flavor:** *"He has never seen a newspaper before. Pick your jaw back up off the floor."*
- **Modifier:** Granddad-tagged AND Kin cards in the same zone this turn capture a SUNDAY COMICS Moment. Bidirectional teaching cards (cards where the Kin teaches the Dad) cost -1 Attention.
- **Storytelling source:** Rapid Reflection: The Funny Papers. The generational-shift moment without nostalgia-weight.

### 5. The Bedtime Bit
- **Flavor:** *"Same story. Same voice for the witch. Same 'oh no, not the witch.' Same kid laughing."*
- **Modifier:** Cards in Your Family with the Holding tag activate twice this turn. Captures a BEDTIME BIT Moment if a Kin is in your Family zone.
- **Storytelling source:** Sunday-comics rule #13 (repetition with variation). The repeated joke as relationship ritual.

### 6. The Color Commentary Year
- **Flavor:** *"And here he comes, eating a bagel, ladies and gentlemen — the man, the myth—"*
- **Modifier:** This turn, when you play any card with the Cheering... wait, *(Cheering isn't a virtue; this Year card lives in the Naming/Welcoming overlap. Better:)* — when you play any card, narrate it (player choice of: name a virtue your card embodies). If correct, capture a COLOR COMMENTARY Moment.
- **Storytelling source:** Mode #1. Voice-of-the-stadium. The kid pretends to hate it.

### 7. The Costume Party (At The DMV)
- **Flavor:** *"He didn't get the email about not wearing the costume to the DMV. He's not changing now."*
- **Modifier:** Cards with the Play cross-tag cost -1 Attention this turn (minimum 0). All Play-tagged cards capture an OUT OF POCKET Moment.
- **Storytelling source:** The mode-#4 specific-weird-hobby dad. Sincere commitment to the bit.

### 8. The Sourdough Year
- **Flavor:** *"The starter has a name. The kid named it. The kid named it Glenn."*
- **Modifier:** Tools attached to a Dad in Your Craft this turn give that Dad +1 Pitch value. Captures a SOURDOUGH Moment.
- **Storytelling source:** Mode #4 verbatim. The hobby-too-long-talked-about as the best part of dinner.

## Tender Years (5 cards — warm, low-key, no joke)

### 9. The Letter Home
- **Flavor:** *"You wrote the postcard from a parking lot. The breakfast knows."*
- **Modifier:** First Long-Distance-tagged play this turn captures a LETTER HOME Moment. Connection cards cost -1 Attention.
- **Storytelling source:** The Long-Distance Dad. The deployed parent. The college kid on the phone.

### 10. The Long Hug
- **Flavor:** *"You held it longer than usual. Nobody had to say why."*
- **Modifier:** Holding-tagged cards in any zone this turn give both their controller and their controller's opponent a small smoothing benefit (each player draws 1 if they have under 4 cards in hand).
- **Storytelling source:** Sunday-comics rule #11 (the punch is a feeling, not a joke). End on an image, not a quip.

### 11. Sitting With Sadness
- **Flavor:** *"You didn't fix it. You weren't supposed to. Anything mentionable is manageable."*
- **Modifier:** Listening-tagged cards capture a SITTING WITH Moment. No Missed-Year flag fires this turn even if you played nothing — *being there is showing up*.
- **Storytelling source:** *For Anyone Who Is Sad* (Masculinity-Detox). Rogers's "anything mentionable is manageable." The For-Anyone-Who-Is-Sad voice.

### 12. The Note In The Lunch
- **Flavor:** *"Same joke every Tuesday. They never said they liked it. They never threw the note away."*
- **Modifier:** Tending-tagged cards activate at +1 strength this turn. Captures a NOTE IN THE LUNCH Moment if you played a Tending card and a Kin is in Your Family.
- **Storytelling source:** Mode #6 (the single dad who packs lunch the night before). Repetition-as-love.

### 13. The Day My Dad Showed Up
- **Flavor:** *"You don't remember what he said. You remember he was there."*
- **Modifier:** Showing-Up-tagged cards entering any zone this turn capture a SHOWED UP Moment. Cards your opponent plays into your zones do NOT count as disruption this turn.
- **Storytelling source:** Brown's recurring phrase, *Daring Greatly*. The most-quoted father-presence phrase in the genre.

## Bumbler-With-Accountability Years (5 cards — sheepish-but-laughing, the distinctive voice)

### 14. The Forgotten Lunch
- **Flavor:** *"You let him learn the natural consequence. You also drove the lunch over at 11:30."*
- **Modifier:** Mending-tagged cards cost -1 Attention this turn. Capture a FORGOTTEN LUNCH Moment if you played a Tending card AND a Mending card this turn.
- **Storytelling source:** Rapid Reflection verbatim. *"Natural consequences are the best thing for improving yourself. It only gets better if I sit in the embarrassment."*

### 15. The Severe Tire Damage Year
- **Flavor:** *"You handled it. Mostly didn't panic. Did not call your wife to come hold your hand."*
- **Modifier:** Resilience faction cards activate at +1 effect this turn. Captures a HANDLED IT Moment.
- **Storytelling source:** Rapid Reflection: Severe Tire Damage verbatim.

### 16. The Garbage Disposal Year
- **Flavor:** *"You don't need everything to be a science experiment. You can just throw it away and take the trash out."*
- **Modifier:** Once this turn, you may discard any number of cards from your hand to gain that much Attention.
- **Storytelling source:** Rapid Reflection: The Garbage Disposal verbatim. The un-glamorous adult move.

### 17. The Wrong Washer
- **Flavor:** *"Replaced the wrong washer. Flooded the basement. Laughed first. Then fixed it."*
- **Modifier:** Tinkerer-tagged AND Mending-tagged cards in Your Craft capture a WRONG WASHER Moment when activated this turn.
- **Storytelling source:** Mode #13. Sunday-comics three-beat: action / complication / quiet payoff.

### 18. The 11:30 Bedtime Compromise
- **Flavor:** *"You said 9. They asked for 10. You said 9:30. Everyone left at 11:30 happy."*
- **Modifier:** First Choosing-tagged play this turn captures a SLEEP NEGOTIATIONS Moment. Adapting-tagged cards activate at +1 effect.
- **Storytelling source:** The Sunday-comics "kid as the straight man" energy with the dad as well-meaning bumbler.

## Civic / Community Years (4 cards — Your Community zone activations)

### 19. The Block Party
- **Flavor:** *"You brought the speakers. Nobody asked. Nobody complained. The kid danced."*
- **Modifier:** This turn, Resonance fires twice in Your Community (instead of once at end-phase, also at start-phase). Captures a BLOCK PARTY Moment if both players have 2+ cards in Your Community.
- **Storytelling source:** Mode #18 (the family playlist) at neighborhood scale.

### 20. Coaching Practice
- **Flavor:** *"You weren't sure they'd listen. They listened. Don't admit you were nervous."*
- **Modifier:** Mentorship... wait, *Mentorship isn't on the locked list. Better:* Teaching-tagged cards in Your Community gain +1 Resonance opportunity this turn.
- **Storytelling source:** Mode #15 (calm-on-the-sideline coach). The opposite of yelling-dad.

### 21. The Field Trip
- **Flavor:** *"You volunteered. You haven't slept. The bus driver asks if you're a parent or a chaperone. The kid claims you twice."*
- **Modifier:** This turn, Connection cards in any zone activate at +1 effect. Capture a FIELD TRIP Moment if a Kin and a Connection-faction Dad are in the same zone.
- **Storytelling source:** Volunteering-as-fatherhood. Mode #16 (the same coffee shop every Saturday) in mobile form.

### 22. The Tutor Hour
- **Flavor:** *"They don't know how to do their math. You don't either. You learn it together."*
- **Modifier:** Bidirectional-teaching cards (where the Kin teaches the Dad) activate twice this turn. Wondering-tagged cards capture a TUTOR HOUR Moment.
- **Storytelling source:** The "I don't know. What do *you* think?" Sunday-comics beat.

## Generational Years (3 cards — your own father, the long line of dads)

### 23. Calling The Old Man
- **Flavor:** *"You called because the kid asked the question you didn't know how to answer. The old man knew. Of course he knew."*
- **Modifier:** When you play a Granddad-tagged card OR a Long-Distance-tagged card this turn, capture a CALLING THE OLD MAN Moment.
- **Storytelling source:** Generational humility — the dad calling his own dad. The chain of fathers.

### 24. His Mother's Birthday
- **Flavor:** *"She loved that song. The kid knew the song before they knew her. That's how it goes."*
- **Modifier:** Once this turn, you may bring a card from your discard back to Your Family at no Attention cost. Captures a HIS MOTHER'S BIRTHDAY Moment.
- **Storytelling source:** The dead grandparent who's still in the family playlist. Tender-not-saccharine.

### 25. The Family Reunion
- **Flavor:** *"Three generations. One backyard. The kid asked who that man was. You told them their great-uncle. They asked again. Long story."*
- **Modifier:** Resonance events fire +1 time per shared tag in Your Family this turn (so a 1-shared-tag pair fires 2 Resonances).
- **Storytelling source:** The complicated-family backyard. Stepfathers, ex-wives, the cousin nobody talks about, the cousin everyone talks about.

## Hard Moments Handled (3 cards — the year that wasn't easy)

### 26. The Quiet Year
- **Flavor:** *"Nobody had anywhere to be. Nobody filled the silence. It was hard. It was good."*
- **Modifier:** Non-Active cards (cards without Activated abilities) activate twice this turn. Cards in Your Family zone are immune to opponent disruption ops.
- **Storytelling source:** *Subtle Shifts* / *For Anyone Who Is Sad*. The year that didn't have a story. The year that healed.

### 27. The Recovery Year
- **Flavor:** *"Some years you come back from. This was one. 'I am not the man I was last year. That is the work.'"*
- **Modifier:** Once this turn, return The Recovery Dad (or any Persisting-tagged Dad) from your discard to a zone of your choice at no Attention cost.
- **Storytelling source:** The Recovery Dad's voice. Owning-without-wallowing. Bumbler-with-accountability at the highest stakes.

### 28. The First Real Talk
- **Flavor:** *"You sat with them for an hour. You didn't have answers. You stayed."*
- **Modifier:** Listening-tagged cards capture a FIRST REAL TALK Moment. Cards in Your Family activate at +1 effect this turn. No Missed-Year flag this turn.
- **Storytelling source:** *Feeling, Finding* (Masculinity-Detox) + Brown's "the story I'm telling myself." The conversation that mattered.

## Distribution / Tone audit

By tonal mode:

| Mode | Count | % | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Joyful | 8 | 29% | Hits the joy-is-structural target |
| Tender | 5 | 18% | Warm-not-saccharine |
| Bumbler | 5 | 18% | The distinctive voice asset |
| Civic | 4 | 14% | Your Community zone activations |
| Generational | 3 | 11% | Multi-generation hooks |
| Hard | 3 | 11% | Earned weight without preachiness |

This 28-card sample is roughly 56% of the planned 50-card deck. The remaining 22 cards (to be authored in M1) should fill in:
- 5-8 more Joyful (Sunday-comics adjacent)
- 4-6 more Tender (small domestic warmth)
- 3-5 more Bumbler-with-accountability
- 2-3 more Civic
- 2-3 more Generational
- 1-2 more Hard

The Year deck is **Dad TCG's storytelling engine**. Every match draws a different 18 Years from these 50, producing emergent narrative texture. The Highlight Reel at end of match captures Moments triggered, including the named Year cards that fired them. The shareable artifact: *"Year 3: The Letter Home. Year 4: Coaching Practice. Year 5: The Quiet Year. Year 6: Hot Lava Living Room…"* That's a story.

## Tone tests applied

- **Rogers test** (could this be said to a child?): all 28 pass. The Recovery Year is the closest call — it earns the gravity, and the kid hearing "*some years you come back from*" gets a true thing.
- **Rapid Reflection test** (lived specificity, not abstract virtue): all 28 ground in concrete actions, named objects, named moments. The bagel narrated like color commentary; the parking-lot postcard; the costume at the DMV; the kid claiming you twice on the field trip.
- **Joy distribution**: at 8/28 explicitly Joyful + many warm/tender carrying joy quietly, the Year deck pulls its share of the parallel-track-joy load.

## Notes for M1

When authoring the remaining 22 Year cards, prioritize:

1. **Reverse-teaching Year cards** — events where the Kin teaches the Dad (the Pokémon lesson, the pronouns conversation, the "you have to apologize for the thing you said" Year).
2. **Stepfather and chosen-family-specific Years** — make sure The Stepdad and The Big Brother Dad have native Year card hooks beyond what they share with biological-dad archetypes.
3. **Mode-#17 Years** (the dad practicing pronouns in the car) — bring queer-dad and ally-dad lived experience into the deck explicitly.
4. **Adoption / fertility / loss Years** — handled with the bumbler-with-accountability voice, never tragic-inspirational.
5. **Cultural-specificity Years** — the framework's "no one way to be a man" demands the Year deck includes Years specific to non-white, non-Western, non-middle-class fatherhood. The voice-research agent flagged these as gaps; the user's *15-Minutes-In-A-Tea-Shop* essay names the principle.
